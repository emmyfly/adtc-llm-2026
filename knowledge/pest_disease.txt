FALL ARMYWORM: Major threat to maize nationwide. Identify: window-pane feeding on leaves, frass in leaf whorl. Control: Ampligo (chlorantraniliprole), neem seed extract, Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt). Spray early morning or late evening. Cultural control: early planting, intercropping with legumes.

CASSAVA MOSAIC DISEASE: Spread by whitefly. Symptoms: yellow/green mosaic pattern on leaves, leaf distortion. No chemical cure. Control: plant resistant varieties (TMS 30572, TME 419), remove infected plants, control whitefly with neem.

RICE BLAST: Caused by Magnaporthe oryzae. Symptoms: diamond-shaped lesions on leaves. Control: resistant varieties, avoid excessive nitrogen, apply Tricyclazole fungicide.

TOMATO LEAF MINER (Tuta absoluta): Devastating pest since 2016 in Nigeria. Mines inside leaves and fruits. Control: pheromone traps, neem oil, Spinosad, remove affected plant parts. Prevention: use insect-proof nets in nursery.

BANANA BUNCHY TOP VIRUS: Affects plantain and banana in southern Nigeria. Spread by aphids. Symptoms: narrow, bunched leaves with dark green streaks. No cure - uproot and burn infected plants, use clean planting material.

AFLATOXIN IN GROUNDNUT/MAIZE: Fungal toxin from Aspergillus flavus. Prevent: dry to below 13% moisture, store in airtight containers, use Aflasafe biocontrol product developed by IITA.
