IRRIGATION IN NIGERIA:
- Fadama farming: Dry season farming in low-lying flood plains. Government Fadama programme provides support.
- Drip irrigation: Most water-efficient. Costs N200,000-500,000 per hectare to install. Best for vegetables and fruit trees.
- Sprinkler: Covers large areas. N300,000-800,000 per hectare. Good for grains.
- Furrow: Cheapest, gravity-fed. Works on sloped land near rivers.
- Solar pumps: N150,000-400,000. Replaces diesel pumps. Saves N50,000+ per season on fuel.

WATER SOURCES:
- Boreholes: Most reliable. Drilling costs N500,000-2M depending on depth and location.
- Rivers/streams: Free but seasonal. Risk of contamination.
- Rainwater harvesting: Collect in tanks during rainy season. 1000L tank costs N15,000-30,000.

DRY SEASON CROPS (with irrigation):
- Tomato, pepper, onion, lettuce, cabbage, cucumber, watermelon
- Fadama areas: rice, maize, wheat (in North)
