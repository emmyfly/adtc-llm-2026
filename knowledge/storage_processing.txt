POST-HARVEST STORAGE:
- Grain (maize, rice, cowpea): Dry to 13% moisture. Use hermetic bags (PICS bags, GrainPro). Cost N500-1000 per bag. Prevents weevil damage without chemicals.
- Cassava: Process within 24 hours of harvest. Make garri, fufu, or cassava flour. Fresh cassava spoils in 2-3 days.
- Yam: Store in well-ventilated yam barns. Treat cut surfaces with wood ash. Can last 3-6 months.
- Tomato: Very perishable. Process into paste, dry, or sell immediately. Solar dryers cost N50,000-150,000.
- Vegetables: Use evaporative coolers (clay pot-in-pot). Extends shelf life 3-5x. Zero electricity needed.

PROCESSING OPPORTUNITIES:
- Cassava to garri: 4 tonnes cassava = 1 tonne garri. Value increases 3x.
- Tomato to paste: Reduce losses from 50% to under 10%. Small processing unit costs N500,000-2M.
- Rice milling: Paddy to milled rice. Small mill costs N1.5-5M.
- Groundnut to oil: Cold press extraction. High demand for pure groundnut oil.
