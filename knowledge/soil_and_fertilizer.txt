NIGERIAN SOIL ZONES:
- Northern Guinea Savanna: ferruginous soils, sandy loam, low organic matter. Need nitrogen and phosphorus.
- Southern Guinea Savanna: transition soils, moderate fertility. Good for yam and maize.
- Derived Savanna: degraded forest soils, need organic matter replenishment.
- Rainforest zone: ferralitic soils, acidic, high iron/aluminum. Good for tree crops.
- Fadama: alluvial flood plains, very fertile, suitable for dry season farming with irrigation.

COMMON FERTILIZERS IN NIGERIA:
- NPK 15-15-15: general purpose, 4-6 bags per hectare for most crops. Cost ~N18,000 per 50kg bag.
- Urea (46-0-0): nitrogen top dressing, 2-3 bags per hectare. Apply at 4-6 weeks after planting.
- SSP (Single Super Phosphate): phosphorus source, good for legumes.
- Organic: poultry manure (richest), cow dung, compost. Apply 5-10 tonnes per hectare.
- Green manure: plant mucuna or centrosema before main crop, plough in.

LIMING: Southern soils often acidic (pH 4.5-5.5). Apply agricultural lime at 1-2 tonnes per hectare to raise pH. Particularly important for maize and vegetables.

SOIL TESTING: Available through state Agricultural Development Programme (ADP) offices, IITA Ibadan, and university soil labs. Cost N5,000-15,000 per sample.
