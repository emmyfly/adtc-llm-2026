CURRENT CROP PRICES (2026 estimates, varies by state):
- Maize: N250,000-350,000 per tonne. Higher in South, lower in North.
- Rice (local): N400,000-550,000 per tonne. Imported rice higher.
- Cassava (fresh tubers): N80,000-120,000 per tonne.
- Garri (processed cassava): N250,000-400,000 per tonne depending on quality.
- Yam (white): N200,000-350,000 per tonne. Peaks December-March.
- Tomato: Highly volatile. N15,000-80,000 per basket depending on season. Cheapest Oct-Feb.
- Cowpea (beans): N400,000-600,000 per tonne.
- Palm oil: N500,000-700,000 per tonne.
- Groundnut: N350,000-500,000 per tonne.

WHERE TO SELL:
- Local markets: Mile 12 (Lagos), Dawanau (Kano - largest grain market in West Africa), Bodija (Ibadan)
- Commodity exchanges: AFEX, Lagos Commodities and Futures Exchange
- Aggregators: Farmcrowdy, ThriveAgric, Babban Gona (for grains)
- Export: sesame, cashew, shea butter have strong export demand
