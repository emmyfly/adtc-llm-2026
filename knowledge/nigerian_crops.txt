CASSAVA: Plant in April-May. Harvest 9-12 months later. Best varieties for Nigeria: TMS 30572, TMS 98/0505, TMS 419. Soil pH 5.5-6.5. Spacing: 1m x 1m. Major diseases: Cassava Mosaic Disease (CMD), Cassava Brown Streak Disease (CBSD), bacterial blight. Control CMD with resistant varieties from IITA Ibadan.

YAM: Plant in February-April in mounds or ridges. Seed yam size: 200-500g. Major species: Dioscorea rotundata (white yam), D. alata (water yam), D. cayenensis (yellow yam). Spacing: 1.2m x 1.2m. Staking improves yield by 30-40%. Store in yam barns with ventilation. Common diseases: yam anthracnose, yam mosaic virus.

MAIZE: Plant at onset of rains. North: June-July. South: March-April. Varieties: SAMMAZ series (drought tolerant), OBA SUPER (high yield). Spacing: 75cm x 25cm. Apply NPK 15-15-15 at 4 bags/hectare. Top dress with urea at 6 weeks. Major pest: fall armyworm - use Ampligo or neem extract.

RICE: Upland rice plant March-April. Lowland/fadama rice plant June-July. Varieties: FARO 44, FARO 52, NERICA. Spacing: 20cm x 20cm. Water management critical - maintain 5cm standing water during tillering. Major diseases: rice blast, brown spot. Apply fungicides preventively.

TOMATO: Plant in dry season (October-March) for best results. Varieties: UC82B, Roma VF, Tropimech. Spacing: 60cm x 45cm. Stake plants at 3 weeks. Common issues: blossom end rot (calcium deficiency), tomato leaf miner, whitefly. Use neem oil for organic pest control.

COWPEA (beans): Plant June-July in North, April-May in South. Varieties: IT97K-499-35, IT99K-573-1-1. Spacing: 75cm x 20cm. Major pest: Maruca pod borer. Storage: triple hermetic bags prevent weevil damage.

OIL PALM: South-South and South-East zones. Tenera variety preferred. 143 palms per hectare. First harvest at 3-4 years. Peak production at 7-15 years. Requires 1500-2000mm rainfall.

COCOA: South-West zone (Ondo, Osun, Ekiti, Ogun). Shade management critical. Spacing: 3m x 3m. Major diseases: black pod disease, swollen shoot virus. Harvest October-March.
